My Interests

My greatest expertise is in the area of languages and intercultural competence – within Europe as well as far beyond Europe. In the same way, I am interested in dialects and their diversity.

In addition to language skills, I am enthusiastic about insights into what languages are embedded in: the manyfold cultural aspects, the knowledge hidden in languages as well as their rational and mythical worlds of ideas. I can deal with this topic at an academic level.

A few examples of what fascinates me: a language that has nine words for the verb to drink; no separate word for animal, but an incredibly extensive anatomical vocabulary. A German dialect with a fifth noun case. Languages without hierarchies. Languages in which swear words are freely used and those in which they are a strict taboo.

  • Particularly fascinating to me are languages from the Tuu und Kx'a language families: the way these languages interact with people and the environment, how these things are perceived from the views of different languages, and in which ways diverse languages provide you with incredible possibilities of expressing yourself. Ju which appears phonemically difficult but has an amazingly easy-to-learn grammar. So far, I have learned two of them:
    • Juǀ'hoan (from the Kx'a language family, spoken in Namibia und Botswana)
    • Taa, also known as ǃXoon (the last truly living member in the Tuu language family, spoken in Botswana und Namibia)
